Literature Survey

Project Scope

The literature survey on the project scope reveals that entertainment review systems, which are primarily developed in Western countries, are often not suitable for non-Western societies due to significant cultural differences. These systems typically reflect the values, norms, and preferences of Western audiences, which do not resonate with the cultural, social, and moral frameworks of countries like Sri Lanka. As a result, non-Western societies, particularly in South Asia, struggle with the applicability and relevance of these systems, leading to widespread neglect and disengagement, especially among younger generations who find little connection to the values promoted by such review models.

In Sri Lanka, this challenge has become increasingly pressing as the consumption of global media continues to rise, particularly through digital streaming platforms. Without a localized entertainment review system that is culturally sensitive and reflects the values of the local community, there is a risk of exposing younger audiences to content that might be inappropriate or misaligned with traditional norms. The current entertainment review mechanisms, largely influenced by global trends, fail to provide adequate guidance for Sri Lankan families, resulting in unsupervised media consumption that could erode cultural values and family cohesion.

Moreover, the younger demographic in Sri Lanka often disregards the existing review systems, perceiving them as out of touch with their preferences and daily media habits. This disconnect between the available review platforms and the needs of local users exacerbates the issue, allowing potentially harmful global entertainment influences to pervade Sri Lankan society unchecked. The lack of effective parental control tools and culturally appropriate review systems only heightens this challenge, further threatening the preservation of local cultural sensitivities.

Given these circumstances, there is a growing need for the development of a robust, localized entertainment review system tailored to Sri Lankan audiences. Such a system would not only protect cultural values but also offer families the tools to regulate and monitor media consumption effectively. It would enable the Sri Lankan community to engage with global media in a way that aligns with their cultural and social context, fostering a healthier and more guided approach to digital entertainment. This localized solution could serve as a model for other non-Western societies facing similar challenges, highlighting the importance of adapting entertainment review systems to meet the unique cultural needs of diverse audiences.

Research Objectives

Our Focus

3. Establish a feedback mechanism

Sub Objectives-

Methodology

A Detailed Research Approach

The methodology for this research encompasses a comprehensive approach to designing a culturally sensitive movie cataloging, reviewing, and recommendation platform tailored to Sri Lankan audiences. It includes a mix of qualitative and quantitative research methods, system design, machine learning integration, and user feedback analysis to address both technological and cultural aspects.

Survey Design and Data Collection

A Google Form survey was developed to gather insights from Sri Lankan households about their media consumption habits, preferences, and challenges. The survey collected both qualitative and quantitative data on how families engage with digital media and their expectations from a localized movie streaming platform. The survey aimed to identify key issues such as communication barriers, parental control needs, and the cultural sensitivities that influence media consumption in Sri Lanka.

Qualitative and Quantitative Data Analysis

Qualitative Analysis: Responses were categorized and analyzed to understand cultural and familial expectations around media. This helped shape the feature design of the platform, ensuring it meets the cultural needs of Sri Lankan families. Quantitative Analysis: Techniques such as regression analysis and clustering were applied to the survey data to identify common patterns in media consumption and the demand for specific features like parental controls and discussion forums.

Development of the Movie Cataloging and Reviewing System

A movie cataloging system was designed specifically for Sri Lankan audiences, taking into account cultural sensitivities. The system includes a framework for movie ratings and reviews based on local preferences, allowing users to categorize content in ways that reflect Sri Lankan values. This feature provides users with more relevant movie information compared to global review platforms, promoting culturally appropriate content.

Sentiment-Enhanced Movie Recommendation System

Using neural networks, a recommendation system was developed to provide personalized movie suggestions based on user preferences and sentiment analysis. The system analyzes both explicit user behavior and the sentiment of movie reviews to improve the relevance of recommendations. This system combines traditional recommendation methods with sentiment scoring to reflect the unique preferences and sensibilities of Sri Lankan users.

Design and Development of a Family-Oriented Social Networking Platform

The platform integrates movie streaming with social networking features designed to enhance family communication. Specific elements, such as topic-based discussions and real-time parental controls, were developed to facilitate healthy family conversations around movies. The platform also includes a bad word detection system, powered by a Random Forest algorithm, to ensure a safe and respectful environment for family discussions.

UI/UX Localization for Sri Lankan Context

Research was conducted to adapt the platform's user interface and experience to the Sri Lankan cultural context. The design focused on ensuring that the platform is user-friendly and culturally relevant to local users. Features such as language localization (Sinhala and Tamil) were incorporated to make the platform accessible and appealing to a broader audience in Sri Lanka.

Implementation and Testing of Machine Learning Models

The machine learning models, including the recommendation system and bad word detection, were trained and tested using real-world data. These models were optimized to ensure they align with the cultural needs of Sri Lankan users. Extensive testing was conducted to validate the accuracy and effectiveness of these models, ensuring that they perform well in live environments.

Scalability and Future Integration

The platform was designed with scalability in mind, ensuring that it can accommodate future enhancements, such as adding more languages and content moderation features. Long-term plans include integrating additional machine learning models for content moderation, expanding the recommendation system, and developing new features to maintain cultural relevance and adaptability.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

In Sri Lankan households, unregulated media consumption through global movie streaming platforms is creating challenges in maintaining family communication and cultural values. Existing platforms, which are largely influenced by Western models, lack the cultural sensitivity, parental control features, and localized content necessary to guide media consumption in a manner that aligns with Sri Lankan traditions. This issue not only affects how families engage with media but also risks exposing younger audiences to inappropriate content without proper supervision or cultural relevance, leading to a disconnect between family members and their media habits.

Solution

To address this problem, the research proposes the development of a family-oriented social networking platform integrated with a movie streaming system, tailored specifically for Sri Lankan audiences. The platform will feature culturally appropriate parental controls, a movie cataloging and reviewing system sensitive to local values, and a sentiment-enhanced recommendation engine using neural networks. Additionally, the platform will include a bad word detection system to monitor and filter inappropriate content in real-time during family discussions. The UI/UX design will be localized to suit Sri Lankan cultural preferences, and the entire system will aim to foster healthier communication and media engagement within families, ensuring safe and culturally aligned media consumption experiences.
